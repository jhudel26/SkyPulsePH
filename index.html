About AQI

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized system used to communicate air pollution levels to public. It ranges from 0 to 500, with higher values indicating worse air quality.

AQI Categories

0-50 Good 😊
51-100 Moderate 😐
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive 😷
151-200 Unhealthy 😰
201-300 Very Unhealthy 😨
301+ Hazardous ☠️

About SkyPulsePH

Our Mission

SkyPulsePH provides real-time air quality and weather data for cities and municipalities across the Philippines. We aim to help Filipinos make informed decisions about their daily activities based on current air and weather conditions.

Our data is updated regularly and presented in an easy-to-understand format with health recommendations for different population groups.

Data Source

Data is provided by Open-Meteo Air Quality API using CAMS global atmospheric composition forecasts.

Open-Meteo offers free and open weather and air quality data for everyone.

Data Sources

Open-Meteo API

All air quality data is provided by the Open-Meteo Air Quality API, which uses CAMS global atmospheric composition forecasts.

Open-Meteo offers free and open weather and air quality data for everyone.

Update Frequency

Air quality data is fetched directly from the Open-Meteo API and cached for 10 minutes to ensure the most current information is available to users.

Health Guidelines

General Population

Good (0-50): Normal outdoor activities

Moderate (51-100): Sensitive individuals should limit prolonged outdoor exertion

Unhealthy (101+): Everyone should avoid outdoor activities

Sensitive Groups

Children, elderly, pregnant women, and individuals with respiratory or heart conditions should take extra precautions.

Consult healthcare providers for specific medical advice related to air quality conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AQI?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized system that reports air pollution levels on a scale from 0 to 500. Higher values indicate worse air quality.

How often is data updated?

Air quality data is fetched from the Open-Meteo API and cached for 10 minutes to ensure the most current information is available.

Is the data free?

Yes, all air quality data on our platform is free for public use, provided by Open-Meteo.
